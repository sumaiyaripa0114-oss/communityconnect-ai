Built for New York City's older adults

One person's situation touches five agencies. This is one place to start.

CommunityConnect AI is a one-stop decision-support and resource-navigation tool that helps frontline workers connect older adults with the services they may need, faster — while keeping final decisions with trained professionals and official agencies. Describe a situation once, in plain language, and see every need it touches: home care, Medicare and Medicaid, food, housing, transportation, prescriptions, caregiver support, and social connection — instead of hunting across a dozen scattered sites.

Needs Navigator

Describe an older adult's situation in plain language — as much or as little as you know. Get back every need it likely touches, the NYC and federal programs worth exploring for each, and what to check next. Grounded only in the sourced library below, spanning home care, Medicare/Medicaid, food, housing, transportation, prescriptions, and caregiver support.

This tool never states that someone qualifies for a program, and it never decides anything on its own. Its job is to make sure a real need doesn't get missed because it wasn't the reason for the call.

Case Note Assistant

Paste rough notes from a client interaction. Get back a structured summary, a follow-up list, and a flag for anything that needs urgent attention or a referral.

This tool never invents facts, diagnoses, or eligibility conclusions. Review every summary before it enters a real case file.

Document Search

Paste text from a real, trusted document — a plan handbook excerpt, an agency notice, an internal policy — and ask a question about it. The answer is grounded strictly in the text you paste, not the sample library or general knowledge.

Only paste documents you're authorized to use, and never paste real client-identifying information. If the pasted text doesn't answer the question, the tool will say so instead of guessing.
Paste the relevant excerpt, not the whole file if it's very long.

Conversation Coach

Describe a conversation you're about to have or one that went sideways. Get a few concrete lines you could say, plus what to avoid — not a script to read verbatim, a starting point.

This coaches communication only. It doesn't tell you what decision to make or what a client is or isn't entitled to.

Why this exists

Through experience in Medicare sales, community outreach, and home care, it became clear how much time frontline workers lose searching across disconnected agencies, PDFs, and program rules to piece together what one older adult actually needs. CommunityConnect AI is built around a simple idea: a worker describes a situation once, in plain language, and gets an organized starting point — every need worth exploring, the programs that might help, what to ask next, and where to verify it — instead of hunting through a dozen scattered sites and hoping nothing important got missed.

What it does not do

CommunityConnect AI does not decide whether someone qualifies for anything. It does not replace a case manager, a benefits counselor, or an official agency determination. Its entire job is to make the search faster and more complete — the decision always stays with a trained professional and the official source.

Core principles

Human decision-making stays central

Every tool surfaces information and options. None of them tell a worker what decision to make.

Grounded responses

The Navigator and Document Search only draw from what's explicitly supplied — never assumption.

Responsible limitations

Every response names what it can't determine: eligibility, coverage, medical, or legal conclusions.

Accessible communication

Plain, compassionate language a worker can relay directly to a client.

Grounded in these sources

The Resource Navigator's library is built directly from four official pages, each cited by name whenever it's used in a response:

  • ACCESS NYC — Home Care Services Program
  • NYC.gov OCHIA — Medicare
  • Medicare.gov — Special Needs Plans (SNP)
  • NYC.gov HRA — Rental Assistance
  • NYC.gov NYC Aging — NY Connects
  • NYC.gov NYC Aging — In-Home Services
  • ACCESS NYC — Senior Citizen Rent Increase Exemption (SCRIE)
  • NYC.gov NYC Aging — Older Adult Center
  • NYC.gov NYC Aging — Transportation
  • NYC.gov NYC Aging — Caregiving
  • NY State Dept. of Health — EPIC prescription assistance

What I'd build next

Connecting Document Search to an organization's own approved document set instead of pasted text, adding source citations to specific passages, supporting multiple languages, adding role-based access, and building real privacy and audit controls before any actual client data touches the tool.

What I'd do differently

Start with the Case Note Assistant, not the Resource Navigator — it's the tool frontline staff would use most often, and testing it first with real (de-identified) note formats would have shaped every other tool's structure much earlier.
